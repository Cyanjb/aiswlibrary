Camera Movements

14 keywords in this category, each one tested and explained in plain language.

Aerial Shot

High drone or helicopter perspective, moving above the world.

Crane Shot

The camera sweeps vertically or in an arc from a raised arm, moving between ground level and overhead.

Dolly In

The camera physically glides toward the subject on a track.

Dolly Out

The camera glides away from the subject, revealing context and often leaving them small in their world.

Dolly Zoom

Dolly and zoom in opposite directions: the subject stays put while the background stretches.

Handheld

Unstabilized camera with natural shake and drift; human, urgent, imperfect.

Long Take

One unbroken shot that runs far longer than expected; no cuts, no escape.

Orbit

The camera circles the subject, holding them centered while the world wheels behind.

Push In

A gentle forward drift toward the subject, subtler than a full dolly move.

Static Hold

The camera does not move at all; the frame is a stage and life happens inside it.

Steadicam

Stabilized floating movement that follows subjects smoothly through space, no track required.

Tilt Up

The camera pivots vertically upward, revealing height or hope.

Tracking Shot

The camera travels alongside a moving subject, keeping pace like a companion.

Whip Pan

A pan so fast the frame smears; snaps attention from one thing to the next.
