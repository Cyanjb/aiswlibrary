Shot Sizes and Angles

11 keywords in this category, each one tested and explained in plain language.

Close-Up

The face fills the frame; every flicker of feeling becomes the story.

Dutch Angle

The horizon tilts off-level; the world is subtly, deliberately wrong.

Establishing Shot

An opening view that tells the audience where and when they are before anything happens.

Extreme Close-Up

A single detail fills everything: an eye, a trembling hand, a turning key.

Extreme Wide Shot

The subject is tiny in a vast environment; landscape as the loudest voice.

High Angle

Camera looks down, diminishing the subject or mapping the scene.

Low Angle

Camera looks up at the subject, lending power, menace, or myth.

Medium Shot

Framed roughly waist-up; the conversational workhorse of cinema.

Over-the-Shoulder

Framed past one character's shoulder onto another; conversation with built-in geography.

POV Shot

The camera becomes a character's eyes; the audience wears their experience.

Wide Shot

Full body visible with generous surroundings; the scene's geography stays honest.
